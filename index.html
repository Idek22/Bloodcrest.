Prologue: Hematopoiesis

September 1st, 1919, The day Yuka Bloodcrest was brought into the world . . .

Yahir Bloodcrest held his son in the air, donning an uncharacteristic grin on his face. Yahir would stare at the boy for a bit, obsessively analyzing the infant as if he were verifying its existence. Yahir would study the boy's eyes, then his nose, then his arms, then his eyes again.

Yahir would continue to stare for a moment before he finally spoke,

“You are the future of Bloodcrest . . . You will lead this family along the path of the progenitor, and your name will go down in history.”

Yahir would hand the infant over to the doctor before making his way towards his wife.

“How is he?” Selina asked in a soft tone, clearly still fatigued from the birth.

“He’s Amazing, Selina. . . “ Yahir would exclaim as he began to pull a chair towards the bed, “Doctors say he’s a perfectly healthy baby boy.”

“I- I want to hold him . . .”

Yahir would snap his fingers at the doctor before motioning for the him to hand the baby to Selina. The doctor would comply, slowly placing the infant in the hands of its mother.

Selina would stare at the infant for a while, tears beginning to well up within her eyes.

“H- He’s Perfect. . . “ Selina would exclaim, before beginning to address the infant directly, “You will face many challenges and setbacks throughout your life, but no matter what, remember this: I love you, Yuka. . . “

Chapter One: Reticulocyte.

Six years would pass, and Yuka would begin to grow. He proved to be an energetic and outgoing child, often running around the estate, causing trouble wherever he went. Typically, these behaviors would be swiftly corrected; however, as the heir to the Bloodcrest estate, he enjoyed a degree of freedom and leniency rarely afforded to others of the lineage. However, beneath the laughter and Mischief, an uncomfortable truth was beginning to take shape. . .

“YUKA, PLEASE SLOW DOWN,” A servent would shout as he chased Yuka throughout the hallways of the estate. “YOU MUST ATTEND YOUR LESSIONS.”

“NO! Those lesions are boring; you can’t make me go!” Yuka would yell as he continued to dash throughout the hallways, desperate to avoid his lesions.

“YUKA PLEASE, Y-YOU CAN HAVE EXTRA DESSERT TONIGHT AT DINNER”

“IM NOT FALLING FOR THAT TRICK AGAIN,” Yuka would exclaim as he jumped down a flight of stairs.

“BE CAREFUL, YOU COU- huff- You could hurt you- huff- Hurt yourself“

As the servant began to tire, Yuka continued to dart across the estate, leaving the servant behind . . .

Eventually, Yuka would come to a stop to catch his breath. “Huff huff, I- I think I lost him. . . “ Yuka began as he looked around, “Took him long enough to give u-”

Yuka's train of thought came to an abrupt halt as his eyes settled on a massive portrait hanging at the center of the room. For a moment, he simply stared. The man depicted within the portrait was his father, although far younger than Yuka had ever known him. He was clad in a pristine military uniform; his chest adorned with countless medals and decorations. His expression was stern and unwavering, carrying the same commanding presence that seemed to silence entire rooms with a singular glance.

Yuka found himself hesitating before taking a step forward. This was the man that everyone expected Yuka to become, a living embodiment of the Bloodcrest name, someone whose accomplishments and actions not only bring renown and honor to himself, but the Bloodcrest lineage itself. Yuka continued to stare at the photo for a while, seemingly lost in thought, until the voice of the servant brought him back to reality.

“YUKAAAAAAAA”

“Uh oh, I forgot- I need to keep going.” But as Yuka began to turn around and prepare to run, he would trip over his own feet and fall face first into the ground . . .

“YUKA- “ The servant would begin as he burst into the room, near breathless from the chase. “Yuka? . . YUKA?”
